The Problem

  • Most book recommendation systems rely on popularity, ratings, or surface-level metadata.
  • They fail to capture why a reader loved a book, such as tone, themes, emotional depth, or writing style.
  • Readers with the same favourite books may enjoy them for very different reasons, but are treated identically.
  • Searching for recommendations often exposes readers to unwanted spoilers through reviews and summaries.
  • This makes discovering new books feel impersonal and risky.
  • Book Buddy solves this by modelling a reader’s taste deeply and recommending novels without spoilers, preserving the reading experience.

How It Works

1. Preference Profiling

User provides three favourite novels which are analysed for themes, tones, writing styles, pacing and audiences.

2. Intelligent Search

A specialised agent searches the web for novels matching the profile.

3. Final Recommendation

The orchestrator selects one novel and explains why it fits the reader.

Architecture

preference_extractor_agent

Extracts genres, themes, tone, and pacing from the user’s favourites.

book_search_agent

Uses Google Search to find novels aligned with extracted preferences.

novel_recommender_orchestrator

Coordinates agents, tools, and memory to produce the final output.
